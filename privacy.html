Privacy Policy

Last updated: 20 July 2026. This policy is written to comply with the Australian Privacy Principles (Privacy Act 1988) and to explain, in plain language, exactly what SeniorsOS does and doesn't do with your information.

In plain English: we only ask for your name and email when you actually submit a form (a suggestion, a donation note, or creating a member account). We never ask for a Medicare number, Centrelink details, date of birth, home address, bank details, or any health information — and if anything on this site ever asks you for those, don't provide them, it isn't us. We don't sell your details, and we don't show you third-party ads.

1. Who this policy covers

This policy applies to seniorsos.online ("SeniorsOS," "we," "us") and covers everyone who visits the site, uses the free toolkit modules, creates a member account, makes a suggestion, or donates. It's written for our two audiences directly — older Australians using the toolkit themselves, and the adult children or carers who often help set it up — so if any part of it is unclear, that's on us to fix, not you. Contact us using the details at the bottom and we'll explain it plainly.

2. The information we actually collect

SeniorsOS is deliberately built to ask for as little as possible. Here is a complete list — nothing is collected beyond what's below:

  • Suggestion form (suggest.html): the name, email address, and message you choose to enter.
  • Donation note (donate.html): the name, email address, an optional dollar amount, and an optional message you enter. This form records a pledge only — no card or bank details are collected by us.
  • Member accounts: if you create a Full Toolkit account, we (via our identity provider, Netlify Identity) hold your email address and an encrypted password. We never see or store your password in readable form.
  • Payment for the Full Toolkit: handled entirely by Stripe. We pass Stripe your email address and an internal account reference so your payment can be matched to your account; we do not collect, see, or store your card number, expiry, or CVC at any point.
  • Standard web server logs: like virtually every website, our hosting provider (Netlify) automatically logs technical information such as IP address, browser type, and pages requested, for security and reliability purposes. We don't use this for tracking or marketing.

You can browse the site, use Scam Shield, and read every toolkit guide without creating an account or handing over any information at all.

3. What we deliberately do not collect

Because this site is aimed at older Australians, we're conscious that a request for the "wrong" kind of information can itself be a scam pattern. So, as a matter of policy, SeniorsOS will never ask you — through this site, by email, or by phone — for your Medicare number, Centrelink Customer Reference Number, tax file number, driver's licence or passport number, full date of birth, bank account or card details, or any health or medical information. If you're ever asked for these in the name of SeniorsOS, please treat it as suspicious and report it to us via the contact details below.

4. Why we use your information

  • To respond to a suggestion or message you've sent us
  • To create and secure your member account, and let you log back in on any device
  • To match a completed payment to the correct member account and unlock the Full Toolkit
  • To follow up on a donation pledge with a way to pay, if you've asked us to
  • To keep the site secure and working properly (e.g. blocking automated spam submissions)
  • To understand, in general terms, which toolkit modules are actually being used, so we can improve them

We do not use your information for any purpose beyond these without asking you first, and we do not use it to make any automated decision that affects you.

5. Cookies and tracking

SeniorsOS does not run third-party advertising trackers, and at present does not use any analytics or tracking cookies. If you log in to a member account, a small essential session cookie is set by our identity provider so you stay logged in — this is necessary for the account to work and can't be switched off while remaining logged in. If we later add a privacy-respecting analytics tool, we'll name it here first and it will not use cross-site tracking or sell data to advertisers.

6. Who we share information with

We don't sell, rent, or trade your personal information to anyone. We only share it with the following organisations, and only for the purpose of running the site:

  • Netlify (hosting, member accounts/identity, and the suggestion and donation forms)
  • Stripe (payment processing for the Full Toolkit)

Each of these providers may store or process data on servers located outside Australia (typically the United States), as is standard for global hosting and payment infrastructure. Both Stripe and Netlify are established providers with their own privacy and security obligations, and we've chosen them partly on that basis. We don't otherwise disclose your information overseas, and we don't disclose it to any government agency except where required by Australian law.

The Shop page also links to third-party retailers, some via affiliate links. Once you click through to a retailer, that retailer's own privacy policy applies to any information you give them — we don't receive or see your order details. See our terms & affiliate disclosure for more on this.

7. Keeping your information secure

We take reasonable technical and organisational steps to protect the information we hold, including relying on Netlify's and Stripe's security infrastructure (encryption in transit, and industry-standard account security for member logins) rather than building our own systems to store sensitive data. No website can guarantee perfect security, but keeping the amount of information we collect to a minimum is itself a deliberate part of how we protect you.

8. How long we keep it

We keep suggestion and donation messages for as long as is reasonably useful for responding to them and improving the site, and delete them on request. Member account details are kept for as long as your account is active, and deleted within a reasonable period after you ask us to close your account. We don't keep information indefinitely "just in case."

9. Children's privacy

SeniorsOS is intended for use by adults — older Australians and their adult children or carers. It is not directed at, and does not knowingly collect information from, children.

10. Your rights: access, correction, and complaints

Under the Australian Privacy Principles, you have the right to:

  • Ask what personal information we hold about you, and request a copy of it
  • Ask us to correct any information that's wrong or out of date
  • Ask us to delete your information or close your account
  • Make a complaint if you think we've mishandled your information

To do any of these, contact us using the details below — if you'd rather have an adult child, carer, or someone you trust make the request on your behalf, that's completely fine. We'll respond within a reasonable time and won't charge a fee for a straightforward request. If you're not satisfied with our response, you can take your complaint to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC), the independent regulator for privacy in Australia.

11. Changes to this policy

If we change how we collect or use information, we'll update this page and change the "last updated" date above. We won't make a material change that reduces your privacy protections without making that change clearly visible on this page.
