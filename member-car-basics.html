Car Basics

Ask about a dashboard light, a strange noise, or a service quote and get a straight answer about what it means and whether it's urgent — before you call anyone.

How it works

Warning lights and mechanic quotes can be genuinely alarming without context. Describe the light, the noise, or the quote, and Claude gives you a plain-English read on what it likely means, how urgent it is, and what a fair service cost looks like — so you're better prepared before speaking to a mechanic.

How to use it

  • Describe the light, sound, or symptom — colour, when it happens, how long it's been going on.
  • Ask whether it's safe to keep driving or whether it needs attention right away.
  • Paste in a service quote and ask if the line items and cost look reasonable.
  • Ask what questions to ask the mechanic before agreeing to any work.

A few prompts to start with

Understand a warning light

"An orange engine-shaped light came on this morning and the car feels the same to drive. Is it safe to keep driving to work, or should I get it looked at today?"

Check a strange noise

"My car makes a grinding noise when I brake, especially at low speed. How urgent is this, and roughly what would a mechanic likely need to fix?"

Sanity-check a service quote

"A mechanic quoted me $780 for a routine service, brake pads, and an oil change on a 2016 sedan. Does that sound reasonable, and what should I ask them to clarify?"

Prepare questions for the mechanic

"My car is due for its yearly service. What are five good questions to ask the mechanic so I understand what I'm actually paying for?"

Copy whichever fits, swap in your own details, and paste it into Claude to get a plain-English answer written for your exact situation.
